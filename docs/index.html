The problem

Photo apps lose the route.
Location apps lose the moments.

Three months later you have a folder of disconnected images, no map, and no idea what the air quality was that day. TravelTrace records all three together — the route, the photos, and the surrounding environment — and lets you replay them as one story.

Features

Built for the whole journey

Every feature serves one goal — making a past trip feel rich enough to relive, not just summarise.

Live GPS tracking

Polyline grows as you walk. Two layers of filtering keep the line smooth even on devices with a partial GMS stack.

Photos pinned on the map

One tap captures the moment and drops a marker exactly where you stood. Multiple shots in the same spot cluster automatically.

Weather & air quality

Logged silently every five minutes from OpenWeatherMap. Temperature, wind, humidity, and AQI all replay along with the route.

Signature

Replay your trip

Drag the slider, tap play, switch to 1× / 5× / 10× / 30×. Photos surface as floating cards the moment the cursor reaches them.

Local-first, cloud-backed

SQLite is the source of truth — recordings survive offline, restarts, and bad networks. Firestore mirrors silently when there's signal.

Light & dark mode

Material 3 theming with a single seed colour. Follows the system appearance — no toggle needed.

How it works

Four taps from start to story

01

Start your walk

Tap Record. Permissions are handled gently — one dialog, one decision.

02

Snap moments

Camera button stays at thumb's reach. Each shot anchors itself to the map automatically.

03

Stop & save

One tap ends the journey. The trip is yours forever — local first, cloud second.

04

Replay later

Drag the slider, watch the journey unfold — and the photos surface exactly where they were taken.
